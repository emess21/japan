Local travel tips

Shinkansen
Always reserve a seat. Unreserved (自由席) can be standing-room on busy days. Book seats at ticket window (みどりの窓口) or online in advance.
Luggage on Shinkansen
Bags over 160cm total must have a reserved luggage space — book at same time as seat. Use Yamato Takkyubin to forward bags between hotels (~¥1,500–2,500/bag, delivered next day).
Suica top-up
Top up at any green JR ticket machine. Look for 「チャージ」. Foreign Visa/Mastercard accepted. Minimum ¥1,000. Keep ¥2,000+ per card.
Kyoto buses
Best way around the city. ¥700 day pass = unlimited rides. Bus 206: Kyoto Station → Gion → Higashiyama. Fare ¥230/ride or Suica.
Tokyo trains
Suica everywhere. Yamanote Line (green loop) connects all major spots. From Ueno: Asakusa 1 stop Ginza metro, Akihabara 1 stop JR, Shinjuku ~20 min.
Taxis
~¥730 start fare. Doors open/close automatically — don't touch them. Most accept IC cards. Show driver destination in Japanese on Google Maps.

Travel tips

🎫
Book train seats now
Shinkansen reserved seats are separate from fares. Book via JR-EAST (Hokuriku) or Smart EX (Tokaido/Sanyo). Buy 2–4 weeks ahead for best availability.
🚃
Suica – physical cards only
Android phones can't use Suica wallet. Use your physical cards (Maks: JE803616121903104, Tenicia: JE806917051601731). Top up at green JR machines — look for チャージ.
💴
Cash is king
Japan is heavily cash-based. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs reliably take foreign cards. Withdraw ¥30,000–50,000 at a time.
🧳
Luggage forwarding (Takkyubin)
Yamato Transport sends bags hotel-to-hotel overnight for ~¥1,500–2,500/bag. Drop at hotel reception or convenience store. Essential for Shinkansen travel days.
🚌

🙇
Etiquette
Bow when greeting. No eating while walking. Carry a bag for rubbish (bins are rare). Quiet on trains. Remove shoes at shrines.
